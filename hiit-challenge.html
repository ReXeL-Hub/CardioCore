4-Week Program

4-Week HIIT Challenge

Transform your cardiovascular fitness with progressive high-intensity interval training

20-30 min/day
4-5 days/week
No equipment
All levels

Program Overview

This 4-week progressive HIIT challenge is designed to rapidly improve your cardiovascular fitness, boost metabolism, and increase endurance. Each week builds on the previous one, gradually increasing intensity and complexity to transform your cardio capacity.

Benefits

  • Burn more calories in less time
  • Increase VO2 max by up to 20%
  • Improve recovery rate between exercise sessions
  • Enhance anaerobic threshold
  • Boost metabolic rate for hours after workout

Weekly Breakdown

Week 1: Foundation

Building baseline fitness with 1:2 work-to-rest ratio

Day 1

Beginner HIIT

  • 5 min warm-up (jogging in place, arm circles)
  • 20 sec Jumping Jacks → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec High Knees → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Mountain Climbers → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Squats → 40 sec rest
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down and stretch
Total: 20 minutes
Day 2

Recovery Cardio

  • 20-minute light jog or brisk walk
  • Focus on controlled breathing
Total: 20 minutes
Day 3

Lower Body HIIT

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 20 sec Squats → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Alternating Lunges → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Squat Pulses → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Glute Bridges → 40 sec rest
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 20 minutes
Day 4

Rest Day

Active recovery - light stretching or walking

Day 5

Full Body HIIT

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 20 sec Jumping Jacks → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Push-ups (modified if needed) → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Squats → 40 sec rest
  • 20 sec Plank → 40 sec rest
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 20 minutes

Week 2: Progressive Overload

Increasing work periods to a 1:1 work-to-rest ratio

Day 1

Intermediate HIIT

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 30 sec Jumping Jacks → 30 sec rest
  • 30 sec High Knees → 30 sec rest
  • 30 sec Mountain Climbers → 30 sec rest
  • 30 sec Squats → 30 sec rest
  • 30 sec Push-ups → 30 sec rest
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 25 minutes
Day 2

Tabata Intervals

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 20 sec Squat Jumps → 10 sec rest (8 rounds)
  • 1 min rest
  • 20 sec Mountain Climbers → 10 sec rest (8 rounds)
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 20 minutes
Day 3

Rest Day

Active recovery - light stretching or yoga

Day 4

Pyramid HIIT

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 30 sec work/30 sec rest for each:
  • Jumping Jacks → High Knees → Burpees → Mountain Climbers → Push-ups
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 25 minutes
Day 5

EMOM (Every Minute On the Minute)

  • 5 min warm-up
  • Minute 1: 15 Squats
  • Minute 2: 10 Push-ups
  • Minute 3: 20 Mountain Climbers
  • Minute 4: 10 Burpees
  • Rest remainder of each minute
  • Repeat for 5 rounds (20 minutes)
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 30 minutes

Week 3: Intensity Push

Challenging your limits with 2:1 work-to-rest ratio

Day 1

Advanced HIIT

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 40 sec Jumping Jacks → 20 sec rest
  • 40 sec Speed Skaters → 20 sec rest
  • 40 sec Mountain Climbers → 20 sec rest
  • 40 sec Burpees → 20 sec rest
  • 40 sec Plank Jacks → 20 sec rest
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 25 minutes
Day 2

Rest Day

Active recovery - light stretching or walking

Day 3

Metabolic Conditioning

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 45 sec Squat Jumps → 15 sec rest
  • 45 sec Push-ups → 15 sec rest
  • 45 sec High Knees → 15 sec rest
  • 45 sec Bicycle Crunches → 15 sec rest
  • 45 sec Burpees → 15 sec rest
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 25 minutes
Day 4

Ladder HIIT

  • 5 min warm-up
  • Burpees: 5-10-15-20-15-10-5 reps
  • Rest 30 sec between sets
  • Mountain Climbers: 10-20-30-40-30-20-10 reps
  • Rest 30 sec between sets
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 25 minutes
Day 5

Cardio Blast

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 45 sec work/15 sec rest:
  • Jumping Jacks → High Knees → Skaters → Squat Jumps → Plank Jacks
  • Repeat circuit 4 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 30 minutes

Week 4: Peak Performance

Maximizing results with varied interval patterns

Day 1

Peak HIIT Challenge

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 50 sec Burpees → 10 sec rest
  • 50 sec Mountain Climbers → 10 sec rest
  • 50 sec Squat Jumps → 10 sec rest
  • 50 sec Push-ups → 10 sec rest
  • 50 sec Plank Jacks → 10 sec rest
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 30 minutes
Day 2

Rest Day

Active recovery - light stretching or walking

Day 3

Descending Intervals

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 60/45/30/15 sec work with 15 sec rest:
  • High Knees → Burpees → Mountain Climbers → Push-ups → Jumping Jacks
  • Repeat circuit 2 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 30 minutes
Day 4

Tabata Finale

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 20 sec max effort/10 sec rest for each:
  • Burpees (8 rounds)
  • 1 min rest
  • Mountain Climbers (8 rounds)
  • 1 min rest
  • Squat Jumps (8 rounds)
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 25 minutes
Day 5

Final Challenge

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 45 sec work/15 sec rest:
  • Jumping Jacks → High Knees → Burpees → Mountain Climbers → Push-ups → Squat Jumps → Plank
  • Repeat circuit 3 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Total: 30 minutes

Tips for Success

Monitor Intensity

Aim to reach 80-90% of your maximum heart rate during work intervals. Use the CardioCore Calculator to determine your target zones.

Prioritize Recovery

Get 7-9 hours of sleep and stick to rest days to allow your body to adapt and grow stronger.

Stay Hydrated

Drink 16-20oz of water before workouts and replenish fluids throughout the day.

Fuel Properly

Consume a light carb and protein meal 1-2 hours before workouts and replenish within 30 minutes after.
