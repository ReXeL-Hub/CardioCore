Heart Health Bootcamp

A balanced approach to cardio training for long-term cardiovascular health and resilience

30-45 min/session
5 days/week
No equipment

Program Overview

The Heart Health Bootcamp is a comprehensive 6-week program designed to strengthen your cardiovascular system, improve heart health markers, and build a solid foundation for lifelong cardiac wellness. Unlike programs focused solely on weight loss or performance, this bootcamp takes a balanced approach by incorporating a variety of cardio modalities that target different aspects of heart health - from endurance and recovery to strength and elasticity.

Benefits

  • Lower resting heart rate by 5-15 bpm
  • Improve heart rate recovery after exertion
  • Reduce blood pressure by 5-10 mmHg
  • Increase stroke volume (blood pumped per heartbeat)
  • Enhance arterial elasticity and circulation
  • Improve VO2 max by 10-15%

Training Philosophy

Zone 2 Training Base

The foundation of this program is moderate-intensity, steady-state cardio in "Zone 2" (60-70% of max heart rate). Research shows this intensity optimizes mitochondrial function, capillary density, and fat oxidation while being sustainable for longer durations.

Strategic High-Intensity Work

Carefully timed high-intensity intervals expand stroke volume, strengthen the heart muscle, and improve the body's ability to clear lactate. Limited to 1-2 sessions per week to prevent overtraining.

Active Recovery Focus

Dedicated low-intensity recovery sessions improve parasympathetic nervous system function, heart rate variability, and vagal tone - key markers of cardiovascular resilience and longevity.

Progressive Adaptation

Gradual increases in volume and intensity allow the cardiovascular system to adapt appropriately, building lasting improvements without excessive stress or inflammation.

6-Week Program Structure

The program uses a periodized approach with heart rate zone training for precise cardiovascular development.

Phase 1: Weeks 1-2 - Foundation Building

Establishing aerobic base and proper form

Monday

Zone 2 Foundation

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 25-30 min steady-state cardio at 60-70% max HR
  • Choose walking, jogging, cycling, or swimming
  • Focus on breathing and maintaining conversation pace
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 2 (60-70% MHR)
Tuesday

Mobility Circuit

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 20 min circuit (40 sec work/20 sec rest):
  • - Arm circles
  • - Hip openers
  • - Bodyweight squats
  • - Gentle side bends
  • - Walking lunges
  • 5 min cool-down stretching
Target: Zone 1-2 (50-65% MHR)
Wednesday

Introduction to Intervals

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 15-20 min:
  • - 1 min slightly increased pace (70-75% MHR)
  • - 2 min easy recovery pace
  • - Repeat 5-7 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Alternating Zone 2-3 (65-75% MHR)
Thursday

Active Recovery

  • 20-30 min very light activity
  • Walking, gentle yoga, or stretching
  • Focus on deep breathing
Target: Zone 1 (50-60% MHR)
Friday

Zone 2 Endurance

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 30-35 min steady-state cardio at 65-70% max HR
  • Slightly longer than Monday's session
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 2 (65-70% MHR)
Weekend

Rest & Outdoor Activity

  • 1 day complete rest
  • 1 day optional outdoor walking, hiking, or swimming
  • Keep heart rate below 65% max
Target: Zone 1-2 (50-65% MHR)

Phase 2: Weeks 3-4 - Capacity Building

Increasing cardio volume and introducing moderate intensity

Monday

Zone 2-3 Progression

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 35-40 min cardio:
  • - 20 min at 65-70% MHR
  • - 10 min at 70-75% MHR
  • - 5-10 min at 65-70% MHR
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 2-3 (65-75% MHR)
Tuesday

Cardiac Circuit

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 25 min circuit (45 sec work/15 sec rest):
  • - Bodyweight squats
  • - Modified push-ups
  • - Marching in place
  • - Side-to-side steps
  • - Standing oblique twists
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 2-3 (65-75% MHR)
Wednesday

Pyramid Intervals

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 25 min interval session:
  • - 1 min at 75% MHR → 1 min recovery
  • - 2 min at 75% MHR → 1 min recovery
  • - 3 min at 75% MHR → 2 min recovery
  • - 2 min at 75% MHR → 1 min recovery
  • - 1 min at 75% MHR → 1 min recovery
  • - Repeat once
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Alternating Zone 2-3 (65-75% MHR)
Thursday

Active Recovery

  • 25-30 min very light activity
  • Walking, gentle yoga, or stretching
  • Heart rate monitoring to ensure staying in Zone 1
Target: Zone 1 (50-60% MHR)
Friday

Endurance Build

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 40-45 min steady-state cardio
  • Maintain 65-70% MHR throughout
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 2 (65-70% MHR)
Weekend

Active Rest & Outdoor Session

  • 1 day complete rest
  • 1 day 30-45 min outdoor cardio activity
  • Maintain Zone 2 (65-70% MHR)
Target: Zone 2 (65-70% MHR)

Phase 3: Weeks 5-6 - Performance & Integration

Optimizing cardiac output and introducing higher intensities

Monday

Zone 2-3 Endurance

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 40-45 min progressive cardio:
  • - 15 min at 65-70% MHR
  • - 20 min at 70-75% MHR
  • - 5-10 min at 65-70% MHR
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 2-3 (65-75% MHR)
Tuesday

Cardiac Power Circuit

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 30 min circuit (40 sec work/20 sec rest):
  • - Squat to overhead reach
  • - Modified push-ups
  • - High knees
  • - Alternating lunges
  • - Mountain climbers (modified if needed)
  • - Lateral shuffles
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 3 (70-80% MHR)
Wednesday

Heart Rate Recovery Training

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 30 min session:
  • - 1 min at 80-85% MHR
  • - 2 min recovery, monitoring how quickly HR drops
  • - Repeat 8-10 times
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Peaks at Zone 4 (80-85% MHR)
Thursday

Active Recovery

  • 30 min very light activity
  • Focus on breathing techniques and mobility
Target: Zone 1 (50-60% MHR)
Friday

Zone 2 Long Session

  • 5 min warm-up
  • 45-50 min steady-state cardio
  • Maintain 65-70% MHR throughout
  • 5 min cool-down
Target: Zone 2 (65-70% MHR)
Weekend

Mixed Cardio & Rest

  • 1 day complete rest
  • 1 day 45-60 min outdoor activity with natural intervals (hills, terrain changes)
Target: Variable (60-80% MHR)

Heart Rate Zone Training Guide

This program uses heart rate zones to precisely target different cardiovascular adaptations. Use our CardioCore Calculator to determine your personal zones based on age and fitness level.

Zone 1: Recovery
50-60% MHR
Active recovery, improved fat metabolism, enhanced capillary density
Zone 2: Endurance
60-70% MHR
Aerobic base building, mitochondrial development, improved fat oxidation
Zone 3: Tempo
70-80% MHR
Improved lactate threshold, increased stroke volume, enhanced aerobic capacity
Zone 4: Threshold
80-90% MHR
VO2 max improvements, increased cardiac output, anaerobic development
Zone 5: Maximum
90-100% MHR
Maximum cardiac output, neuromuscular power (used sparingly in this program)

Tracking Your Progress

Monitor these metrics throughout the 6-week program to evaluate improvements:

Resting Heart Rate

Measure first thing in the morning before getting out of bed. A decreasing trend indicates improved cardiac efficiency.

Heart Rate Recovery

After a hard effort, time how long it takes for your heart rate to drop by 20 beats. Faster recovery indicates improved cardiac fitness.

Workout Intensity vs. Perceived Effort

Note how hard a workout feels (RPE 1-10) compared to your heart rate. As fitness improves, the same heart rate will feel easier.

Distance/Time at Zone 2

Track how far or how long you can go while maintaining Zone 2 (65-70% MHR). Improvements here indicate enhanced aerobic efficiency.

Heart Health Optimization Tips

Monitor HRV

If possible, track Heart Rate Variability (HRV) using a compatible chest strap or watch. Rising HRV indicates improved cardiac resilience.

Nasal Breathing

Practice nasal-only breathing during Zone 1-2 workouts to enhance oxygen utilization and parasympathetic activation.

Cold Exposure

Consider brief cold showers (30-60 seconds) after workouts to stimulate vagal tone and circulatory adaptation.

Anti-Inflammatory Diet

Focus on omega-3 rich foods, colorful vegetables, and limiting processed carbohydrates to support vascular health.
